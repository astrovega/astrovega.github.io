About me

I am an astrophysics researcher with a dedicated focus on galaxy formation and evolution, galaxy clusters, and gravitational lensing, using both observational and theoretical approaches. I employ artificial intelligence in various astrophysics fields, aiming to comprehend the physical processes that drive galaxy evolution and the relationship between galaxy morphology and environment.

Currently, I am a tenure-track researcher at CEFCA (Teruel, Spain) working on spatially resolved galaxy properties and low surface brightness structures. Between 2011 and 2015, I did my PhD at the Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (Spain) and the Observatoire de Paris (France) on the strong gravitational lensing effect produced by galaxy clusters. Then, I moved to Philadelphia (Pennsylvania, USA), where I spent three years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Pennsylvania (2016-2019). Back in Spain, between 2018 and 2023, I was a postdoctoral researcher as part of IFCA-CSIC (Santander, Spain), IIIA-CSIC (Barcelona, Spain), IAC (Tenerife, Spain), and Universidad de Valladolid (Valladolid, Spain).

Research

My research focuses on galaxy formation and evolution, galaxy clusters, and gravitational lensing from both observational and theoretical perspectives. I am particularly committed to applying artificial intelligence in various fields of astrophysics.

The overarching aim of my research is to comprehend the physical processes driving galaxy evolution over cosmic time and to understand the relationship between galaxy morphology and environment. This is achieved by studying the structure and morphology of galaxies in a broad redshift range through advanced Machine Learning techniques and the gravitational lensing effect produced by galaxy clusters.
